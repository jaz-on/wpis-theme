How a claim gets in.

Six steps from a quote you noticed somewhere on the web to a validated entry in this database. Humans and a bit of AI share the work.

1

You spot a claim

Someone online says something about WordPress. A tweet, a blog post, a Reddit thread, a video comment. You think it belongs here.

→ Or one of our bots finds it on Mastodon or Bluesky automatically.

2

You submit it

Paste the text, drop a screenshot or share the link. No account needed. We extract what’s useful: the claim itself, the platform, the language, and strip out personal details.

→ Screenshots are deleted after extraction. Only the domain is stored, never the full URL.

3

AI pre-tags it

An AI suggests a sentiment, a claim type and a translation. It also looks for similar claims already in the database, to avoid duplicates. None of this is final: it’s a starting point.

4

A human validates

Every submission — yours, a bot’s, someone else’s — lands in a moderation queue. A human reads it, checks the AI’s work, merges duplicates, edits if needed.

→ Yes, one person does this for now. Yes, it’s slow on purpose.

5

It goes live

Once validated, the claim joins the database. It gets a counter that rises each time someone resubmits the same idea. It’s linked to opposing views from other contributors, so readers see the full tension.

6

You can track it

If you created an account, you’ll see your submissions in your private profile: validated, merged, rejected or still pending. No public profiles, no leaderboards, no social clout.
